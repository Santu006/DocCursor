MUTUAL NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT

This Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into as of January 15, 2024 (the "Effective Date"), by and between:

ABC Manufacturing Corp., a Delaware corporation ("ABC"), and

NovaTech Controls Inc., a California corporation ("NovaTech").

Each may be referred to as a "Party" and together as the "Parties."

1. PURPOSE

The Parties wish to explore a potential OEM supply and integration relationship (the "Purpose") and may disclose Confidential Information to each other in connection with the Purpose.

2. DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

"Confidential Information" means non-public information disclosed by either Party, whether in writing, orally, or by inspection, that is marked confidential or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential, including: product roadmaps, pricing, customer lists, technical specifications, firmware source code excerpts, and business plans.

Exclusions: Information that (a) is or becomes public through no fault of the receiving Party; (b) was rightfully known before disclosure; (c) is independently developed; or (d) is rightfully received from a third party without restriction.

3. OBLIGATIONS

3.1 The receiving Party shall use Confidential Information solely for the Purpose and shall not disclose it to third parties except to employees and advisors with a need to know who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as this Agreement.

3.2 The receiving Party shall protect Confidential Information using at least the same care it uses for its own similar information, and in no event less than reasonable care.

4. TERM AND SURVIVAL

4.1 Term. This Agreement continues for two (2) years from the Effective Date unless earlier terminated by either Party upon thirty (30) days' written notice.

4.2 Survival. Confidentiality obligations survive for three (3) years after termination or expiration of this Agreement; trade secrets survive for so long as they remain trade secrets under applicable law.

5. RETURN OR DESTRUCTION

Upon written request, or upon termination, the receiving Party shall promptly return or securely destroy Confidential Information and certify destruction in writing within ten (10) business days, except for copies retained under automated backup systems that are not readily accessible, which remain subject to this Agreement.

6. NO LICENSE; NO OBLIGATION TO PROCEED

No license under any intellectual property is granted. Neither Party is obligated to enter into any further agreement.

7. GOVERNING LAW

This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Delaware. Disputes shall be resolved in the state or federal courts of Wilmington, Delaware.

8. REMEDIES

The Parties agree that monetary damages may be inadequate and that the disclosing Party is entitled to seek injunctive relief for threatened or actual breach, without posting bond to the extent permitted by law. In addition, unauthorized disclosure of firmware source code excerpts shall entitle the disclosing Party to liquidated damages of Fifty Thousand Dollars ($50,000) per incident, as a reasonable pre-estimate of harm and not as a penalty.

Agreed and accepted as of the Effective Date.
